Shrimp scampi is a seafood dish made of shrimp cooked in a butter, garlic, and white wine sauce. This recipe can be made in many ways. Everyone has a different version, but scampi usually includes these three ingredients. You can serve shrimp scampi by itself as an appetizer or over pasta as a main dish.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp
  • Pasta
  • Butter
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Shallots and garlic
  • White wine
  • Lemon juice
  • Seasonings
  • Parsley

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons butter with 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium-high heat. Add shallots and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until shallots are soft.
  3. Add shrimp to the skillet and season with salt and pepper. Cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove shrimp from skillet and set aside.
  4. Add white wine and lemon juice to the skillet and bring to a simmer. Stir in remaining 2 tablespoons butter until melted and combined.
  5. Stir in cooked pasta and shrimp until heated through and well combined. If the mixture is too dry, add more butter as needed; season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with parsley, if desired.
